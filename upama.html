Upama

"South Indian Special"

Previous Next

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Semolina (Sooji or Rava) 2 tbsp Oil or Ghee
    1 tsp Mustard Seeds
    1 tsp Urad Dal (black gram)
    1 tsp Chana Dal (split chickpeas)
    1/2 inch Ginger - finely chopped
    1-2 Green Chilies - finely chopped
    A handful of Cashews (optional)
    1/2 cup finely chopped Onions
    1/4 cup finely chopped Carrots
    1/4 cup Peas
    2.5 cups Water
    Salt - to taste
    A pinch of Asafoetida (hing)
    Fresh Coriander Leaves - for garnish
    Lemon wedges - for serving (optional)

    Process:
    • Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
      Add urad dal and chana dal. Fry until they turn golden brown.
      Add chopped ginger, green chilies, and cashews (if using). Sauté for a minute.
      Add finely chopped onions and cook until they become translucent.
      Add chopped carrots and peas. Cook for another couple of minutes.
      Add semolina (rava or sooji) to the pan. Roast it on medium heat until it turns golden brown and gives a nutty aroma.
      In a separate pot, boil 2.5 cups of water.
      Gradually add the hot water to the roasted semolina, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
      Season with salt and add a pinch of asafoetida. Mix well.
      Cover the pan and let it simmer on low heat for a few minutes until the upma absorbs the water and becomes fluffy.
      Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
      Serve hot upma with coconut chutney or a side of pickle. Enjoy your nutritious and flavorful breakfast!
